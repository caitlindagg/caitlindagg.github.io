About Me

Hi, I'm Caitlin Dagg, a PhD candidate applying my background in computer science to ecosystem restoration, biodiversity conservation, and landscape-scale ecological analysis. My research focuses on the Cumberland Plain, where I use remote sensing of vegetation structure to understand biodiversity and ecosystem resilience at a landscape-scale in order guide restoration efforts.

Research

Here are some of the areas I'm currently working on:

  • Vegetation structure and biodiversity on the Cumberland Plain
  • Conservation prioritisation and restoration strategies
  • Using remote sensing data (LiDAR) for ecosystem analysis

Future

I'll be starting my search for a postdoc position from mid 2025. I'm interested in these areas:

  • Landscape-scale conservation
  • Connectivity
  • Conservation in working landscapes & agroecology
